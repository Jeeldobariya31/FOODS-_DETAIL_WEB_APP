Classic Cincinnati Skyline Chili

Experience the unique flavor of Cincinnati with this classic Skyline Chili recipe, perfect for serving over spaghetti or hot dogs!

Chili Cincinnati Beef Comfort Food
Prep: 15 mins
Cook: 1-3 hrs
Total: 1.25-3.25 hrs
Serves: 6-8

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds lean ground beef (96% / 4% blend recommended)
  • 1-2 cups chopped onion (optional, some recipes include it in the chili, others as a topping)
  • 4-5 cups beef broth or water
  • 1 (6-ounce) can tomato paste or 2 cups crushed tomatoes
  • ¼ cup chili powder
  • 1-2 teaspoons ground cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder or 2 teaspoons instant minced garlic or 4 cloves minced garlic
  • ½ teaspoon onion powder or 1 teaspoon onion salt (if not using fresh onion)
  • ¼ teaspoon ground allspice
  • ¼ teaspoon ground sage or ground cloves
  • ½ teaspoon chipotle flakes or cayenne pepper (or to taste)
  • ¾-1 ¼ teaspoon salt
  • ½ teaspoon granulated sugar
  • ½ ounce unsweetened baking chocolate or 2-3 tablespoons cocoa powder
  • 2 tablespoons apple cider vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce (optional)
  • 1 bay leaf (optional)

Instructions

  1. Brown the Beef (and Onion): In a large skillet or Dutch oven, brown the ground beef over medium-high heat. If using fresh onion, add it with the beef and sauté until translucent. Drain any excess fat. Some recipes suggest not browning the meat first, but rather adding it raw to the liquid and breaking it up as it cooks to achieve a finer texture.
  2. Combine Ingredients: Transfer the browned beef (if applicable) to a large pot or slow cooker. Add the beef broth (or water), tomato paste (or crushed tomatoes), chili powder, cinnamon, cumin, garlic powder, onion powder (or salt), allspice, sage (or cloves), chipotle flakes (or cayenne pepper), salt, sugar, unsweetened baking chocolate (or cocoa powder), apple cider vinegar, Worcestershire sauce (if using), and bay leaf (if using). Stir well to combine.
  3. Simmer: Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to medium-low or low. Simmer uncovered for 1 to 3 hours, stirring occasionally, until the sauce has thickened considerably. Some recipes recommend simmering for 1 hour, while others suggest 3 hours or even 12 hours on low in a slow cooker.
  4. Refine and Rest (Optional but Recommended): Remove the bay leaf (if used). If desired, you can use a potato masher, whisk, or even an immersion blender to break up the meat into very fine pieces, which is characteristic of Skyline Chili. For best flavor, many recipes recommend cooling the chili and refrigerating it overnight, then skimming off any solidified fat before reheating and serving.
