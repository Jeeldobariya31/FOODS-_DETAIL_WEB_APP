Beef and Broccoli Stir Fry

A classic Chinese-American dish, this beef and broccoli stir fry is quick, flavorful, and perfect for a weeknight meal.

Stir-Fry Beef Dinner Quick
Prep: 15-20 mins
Cook: 10-15 mins
Total: 25-35 mins
Serves: 2-4

Ingredients

  • For the Beef:
  • 1 to 1.25 lbs flank steak or sirloin, thinly sliced against the grain
  • 1 teaspoon soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon Chinese rice cooking wine or dry sherry (optional)
  • 1/2 teaspoon cornstarch
  • 1/8 teaspoon black pepper
  • For the Broccoli:
  • 3/4 to 2 cups broccoli florets
  • For the Sauce:
  • 1/4 cup beef broth or water
  • 1/4 cup oyster sauce
  • 1-2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1-2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon toasted sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon minced fresh ginger
  • 1-2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch (dissolved in 1 tablespoon cold water to create a slurry)
  • For Cooking:
  • 1-2 tablespoons high-heat cooking oil (vegetable, canola, or sesame oil)
  • Salt and pepper to taste (for seasoning beef)
  • Optional: Toasted sesame seeds for garnish

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Beef: In a medium bowl, combine the thinly sliced beef with 1 teaspoon soy sauce, 1 teaspoon Chinese rice cooking wine (if using), 1/2 teaspoon cornstarch, and 1/8 teaspoon black pepper. Mix well and let it marinate for at least 10-15 minutes while you prepare other ingredients.
  2. Prepare the Broccoli: You can blanch or steam the broccoli florets until crisp-tender, about 2-3 minutes. Drain thoroughly. Alternatively, you can stir-fry them directly.
  3. Make the Sauce: In a small bowl, whisk together the beef broth, oyster sauce, soy sauce, brown sugar, sesame oil, minced ginger, and minced garlic. Set aside. In a separate small bowl, dissolve 1 tablespoon of cornstarch in 1 tablespoon of cold water to create a cornstarch slurry.
  4. Cook the Beef: Heat 1 tablespoon of high-heat cooking oil in a large frying pan or wok over medium-high to high heat until shimmering. Add the marinated beef in a single layer, working in batches if necessary to avoid overcrowding the pan. Cook for 1-2 minutes per side until browned and no longer pink. Remove the beef from the pan and set aside.
  5. Stir-fry Broccoli (if not blanched/steamed): If you didn't blanch the broccoli, add 1 teaspoon of oil to the pan (if needed) and stir-fry the broccoli florets for 3-5 minutes until tender-crisp.
  6. Combine and Sauce: Return the cooked beef to the pan with the broccoli. Give the prepared sauce a quick whisk and pour it over the beef and vegetables. Bring the sauce to a simmer.
  7. Thicken the Sauce: Stir in the cornstarch slurry. Cook and stir continuously for about 1 minute, or until the sauce thickens to your desired consistency.
  8. Serve: Serve immediately over hot steamed rice, if desired. Garnish with toasted sesame seeds.
