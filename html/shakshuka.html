Classic Shakshuka

Shakshuka is a popular Middle Eastern and North African dish featuring eggs poached in a simmering, flavorful tomato sauce. It's perfect for breakfast, brunch, or a light dinner.

Breakfast Vegetarian One-Pan Middle Eastern
Prep: 10 mins
Cook: 20-25 mins
Total: 30-35 mins
Serves: 2-4

Ingredients

  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 1-2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, deseeded and chopped (optional, but recommended)
  • 1 (28 ounce) can crushed tomatoes (or diced tomatoes, blended)
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp paprika (smoked paprika for extra flavor)
  • 1/4 tsp cayenne pepper (optional, for heat)
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste
  • 4-6 large eggs
  • Fresh parsley or cilantro, chopped, for garnish
  • Crumbled feta cheese (optional, for serving)
  • Crusty bread or pita, for serving

Instructions

  1. Saut√© Aromatics: Heat the olive oil in a large, deep skillet or cast-iron pan over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and cook until softened, about 5-7 minutes. Add the chopped bell pepper (if using) and cook for another 5 minutes until tender. Stir in the minced garlic and cook for 1 minute more until fragrant.
  2. Build the Sauce: Stir in the cumin, paprika, and cayenne pepper (if using). Cook for 1 minute, stirring constantly, until fragrant. Pour in the crushed tomatoes. Season generously with salt and black pepper. Bring the sauce to a gentle simmer, then reduce the heat to low, cover, and let it cook for 10-15 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld and the sauce to thicken slightly. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking.
  3. Add Eggs: Create 4-6 small wells in the simmering tomato sauce using the back of a spoon. Carefully crack an egg into each well.
  4. Poach Eggs: Cover the skillet and continue to cook on low heat for 8-12 minutes, or until the egg whites are set and the yolks are still runny (or cooked to your desired doneness).
  5. Garnish and Serve: Remove from heat. Garnish with fresh chopped parsley or cilantro. If desired, sprinkle with crumbled feta cheese. Serve immediately with crusty bread or pita for dipping into the delicious sauce and runny yolks.
